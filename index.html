Human Agency & AI in Modern Data Work

A practical guide to extracting meaning from data while keeping humans accountable and in control in an AI‑assisted world.

Introduction

AI is becoming a default layer in everyday work. This eBook focuses on how to think clearly about data, KPIs, and analysis when AI tools are always present.

Chapter 1 — Human Agency & the AI Data Strategist

Human agency in the age of AI

As AI tools make execution easier, judgment becomes the scarce skill. Human agency is the ability to define goals, frame questions, and take responsibility for decisions influenced by data.

Prompting as strategy

Prompting is not about clever wording. It is about expressing intent: what decision matters, what assumptions apply, and what uncertainty remains.

Responsibility and trust

AI output should be treated as a draft, not a truth. Humans remain accountable for validation and interpretation.

Exercises

  • Rewrite a vague prompt as a strategic one.
  • Identify one risk of trusting AI output too quickly.

Chapter 2 — From Data to Information

What data is

Data is recorded observation. On its own, it has no meaning. Meaning emerges only when humans ask questions of it.

Descriptive statistics

Descriptive statistics summarise data so humans can reason about it: counts, averages, ranges, and variation.

KPI analysis

KPIs are metrics chosen to reflect goals. What makes them powerful is intent, not calculation.

AI, Copilot, and agents

AI accelerates analysis but does not decide what matters. Human agency remains central.

Exercises

  • Define one KPI that truly reflects success.
  • List statistics you’d check before trusting AI insight.

Chapter 3 — Exploratory Data Analysis

What EDA is

EDA is structured curiosity. It helps you understand what the data is actually saying before drawing conclusions.

A simple EDA workflow

Inspect structure, check quality, explore distributions, compare segments, and log follow‑up questions.

EDA with AI

AI can assist exploration, but humans must verify and interpret.

Exercises

  • Write five EDA questions for a dataset you know.
