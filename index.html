About Learn Data Analysis With Zee

The Learn Data Analysis with Zee initiative is focused on enlightening people about modern data work while staying grounded in the principles of traditional data analysis. It connects established analytical thinking with contemporary tools, practices, and ways of working, helping learners understand both the evolution of data analysis and its practical application today.

This initiative is free to access and is not registered, accredited, or formally certified. It exists purely in an educative spirit — to encourage curiosity, critical thinking, and skill development for anyone interested in learning more about data analysis, regardless of background or experience.

The material is intended to be used constructively: to learn, practise, reflect, and discuss. Learners are welcome to apply the ideas to their own projects and learning journeys, while using sound judgement and ethical practice.

In a friendly but professional spirit, the content should not be mis-used, misrepresented, or presented as original work. It is shared in the spirit of learn, practise, create - learn from this material, practise the ideas, and then go and build something of your own. Natural copyrights apply, as they do to all original educational material, maintaining the work remains respected, credited, and sustainable for the wider learning community.

The Emerging Role Of An AI Data Strategist

The AI Data Strategist role is inherently cross‑industry where the analytical mindset remains consistent and actionable insight achieved from raw data relies on a Human-AI collaboration.

Why Telephonic Conversation Data

Telephonic conversation data in fact adds context to structured data such as product sales. Sales figures show what happened, but conversations explain why it happened.

Benefits of analysing calls data before sales data, are:

  • Drivers behind purchasing behaviour
  • Reasons for churn, hesitation, or repeat buying
  • Customer sentiment and unmet needs

and as a result, product data, is interpreted more accurately, enabling informed decisions.

Data Analysis As The Core Approach

Data analysis is the foundation of working with AI. Its purpose is to support informed decision‑making by extracting meaningful insights from data using clear, repeatable methods. Without this discipline, AI outputs lack reliability and contextual relevance.

The AI Data Strategist prioritises preparing and organising data rather than building or repairing systems. In data analysis, the focus is on reading, understanding, and contextualising data—not on engineering tasks such as schema redesign.

The goal is to make data ready for AI generation. Well‑prepared, well‑organised data enables AI to produce insights that are accurate, contextual, and actionable.

Working Alongside AI Agents and Copilots

As AI agents—such as AI receptionists or service assistants—become more embedded in business operations, human staff must become comfortable working with AI copilots.

Only by working daily with AI copilots can humans develop the practical judgement needed to allow AI agents to “get on with their jobs” while they focus on theirs.

Human-AI collaboration requires:

  • Humans must know when to intervene and when to let AI agents operate independently
  • Clear role boundaries between human work and AI work must be established
  • Human staff work during peak hours and weekdays and AI agents, off-peak hours and weekends.

The Calls Log as the AI Data Strategist’s Starting Point

content

Field Role / Purpose
id Unique identifier for each call. Anchors the call to FAQs, scripts, guides, and artefacts, enabling traceability.
number Phone number associated with the interaction. Supports continuity and identification of repeat interactions.
time Date and time the call occurred. Used to analyse demand patterns and timing behaviour.
call type Nature of the call (inbound, outbound, follow‑up, internal). Helps determine handling logic.
call status Technical outcome of the call (answered, missed, voicemail, abandoned). Highlights service gaps.
contact type Identifies the relationship of the caller to the organisation (customer, partner, internal).
name Name of the contact if available. Supports personalisation and continuity.
intent What the caller is trying to achieve. Core semantic field for routing, FAQs, and scripts.
purpose Specific reason behind the call. Adds nuance to intent.
follow up Indicates whether further action is required and what that action is.
working day Indicates whether the call occurred during standard operating hours.
status Business outcome of the call (open, resolved, escalated, pending).
FAQ Frequently asked question derived from this call or similar calls.
answer Approved response to the FAQ. Acts as trusted knowledge.
script Structured conversation flow generated from calls and FAQs.
guide Supporting guidance such as tone, escalation rules, or compliance notes.
artefact Reusable output created from the call (FAQ, script, prompt, guide, report).
