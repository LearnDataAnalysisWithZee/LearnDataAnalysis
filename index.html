What is a profiled data value?

A profiled data value is a specific piece of information that is deliberately selected and highlighted for closer investigation. This happens while studying records in a spreadsheet or database, or while reviewing information from sources such as an article, a section of a report, a chapter of a book, website content, a telephone conversation, or even a direct interaction with a person, such as a client.

How to Ascertain What a Good Data Value to Choose and Highlight Is?

For a data value to be chosen and highlighted, data must already exist, be in the process of being recorded, or be planned for recording, and this recorded data provides the foundation from which meaningful values can be identified for closer attention.

The frequency with which a data value appears often signals its importance. When a value occurs repeatedly, naturally the investigator’s awareness increases and closer examination is sought after. Choosing and highlighting such a value helps maintain focus on the original purpose of the investigation and makes sure that attention remains aligned with why the data is being analysed in the first place.

Keeping data value profiling in mind from the very beginning acts as a useful technique, and helps with that particular data value being profiled to not only be remembered, but actively followed through as the investigation progresses.
