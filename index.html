Performance Register Dataset

  1. Object{s} Of Analysis - Performance
  2. Subject{s} Of Analysis - Demand & Comparison
  3. GDPR Compliance
  4. Stakeholders & Their Particular Requirements

When Considering Data Analysis, Is Important To Become Comfortable With Client Demand.

'A Data Analysts Mindset On The Job Must Be Client-Oriented On Behalf Of Their Contractor/Employer'.

Data, Seems As Is Everywhere, When The Client Introduces The Data Analyst To The Data To Be Analysed, Which May Be In More Than One Place.

To Officially Recognise The Clients Demand, Classes Of Objects Can Be Created That Categorise The Main Task Flow Of The Client.

'If A Client Is A Cricket Equipent Supplier, A Class Of Objects Could Be, Products, Or Maybe Even, Operational Expenses'.

Once The Object{s} Of Analysis Are Known, Then Satisfying The Clients (Who Is Always A Major Stakeholder) Requirement, Is All Of A Sudden, Realistic.

As Mentioned Earlier, The Data That Needs To Be Analysed Can Be In More Than One Place, Which Can Be Thought Of As, 'Various Data Sources'.

The Performance Register Dataset Is A Unified Dataset, Consisting Of Data Imported From These, 'Various Data Sources'.

The Workflow Of A Data Analyst Must Demonstrate Full Compliance With The GDPR And Respect These Principles, Each Piece Of The Puzzle, In Turn Helps Carve An Helpful And Fruitful Data Analytics Project.
